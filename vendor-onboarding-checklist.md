# Vendor Onboarding Checklist

**Cartagena POAP Route — Checkpoint Host Guide**

Welcome to the Cartagena POAP Route. This checklist covers everything you need to successfully host a checkpoint and help participants claim their POAP.

---

## Section 1 — Before the Checkpoint Goes Live

### Administrative
- [ ] Vendor agreement signed and returned to Cartagena Onchain
- [ ] Checkpoint operating hours confirmed and submitted (must align with QR rotation schedule)
- [ ] Designated staff member identified as the checkpoint contact person
- [ ] Emergency contact information exchanged with Cartagena Onchain

### Technical Setup
- [ ] QR code / printed materials received from Cartagena Onchain
- [ ] QR placement location confirmed (visible, accessible, weather-protected if outdoors)
- [ ] Backup QR code stored securely (for use if primary is damaged)
- [ ] QR display method confirmed (printed A4/A5 sign, display stand, window cling, etc.)
- [ ] Pulse verification link or app access confirmed for any staff who will assist participants

### Training Completed
- [ ] Staff member(s) trained on: what POAP is and how claiming works
- [ ] Staff member(s) trained on: what to do if a participant has trouble claiming
- [ ] Staff member(s) trained on: QR custody rules (see Section 2)
- [ ] Staff member(s) trained on: how to report an incident to Cartagena Onchain
- [ ] Staff member(s) have reviewed the participant FAQ (provided separately)

---

## Section 2 — QR Custody Rules

These rules exist to protect the integrity of the route and ensure fair participation.

**DO:**
- Display the QR code prominently during your confirmed operating hours
- Replace the QR with the updated version when notified by Cartagena Onchain (rotating codes will be sent on the schedule communicated at onboarding)
- Store backup QR codes in a secure location (drawer, envelope, not public-facing)
- Direct participants who have trouble to the self-help guide or contact Cartagena Onchain

**DO NOT:**
- Post or share QR codes on social media, WhatsApp groups, or any online channel
- Display QR codes outside of your confirmed operating hours
- Claim POAPs to your own wallet or the wallet of any staff member from your checkpoint's QR code
- Allow anyone to photograph the QR code for the purpose of sharing it remotely
- Transfer or delegate QR custody to non-authorized parties without notifying Cartagena Onchain

## Section 3 — Helping Participants

### Participant Steps (for reference)
1. Participant scans the QR with their phone camera or wallet app
2. A claim link opens in their mobile browser
3. They connect their wallet (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, etc.) and claim the POAP
4. The POAP appears in their wallet — the process is complete

### Common Participant Questions

**"I don't have a crypto wallet — can I still participate?"**
Participants need a basic EVM-compatible wallet to claim POAPs. Cartagena Onchain provides a simple wallet setup guide (provided separately). Direct participants to it or to the Cartagena Onchain support contact.

**"My POAP didn't appear in my wallet."**
Ask them to check their wallet's POAP section or visit the POAP app. Transactions may take a minute to confirm. If the issue persists, direct them to the Cartagena Onchain support contact.

**"The QR code doesn't scan / the link doesn't work."**
Check whether the QR code is the current rotated code. If not, use the backup. If both fail, contact Cartagena Onchain immediately.

## Section 4 — Incident Reporting

If you observe or suspect any of the following, contact Cartagena Onchain immediately:

- Someone photographing the QR code without claiming (possible intent to share)
- An unusually high number of people claiming in a very short time period
- Someone presenting what appears to be a digitally altered or copied QR code
- Suspected abuse by a staff member or associate
- QR code physically damaged or removed
